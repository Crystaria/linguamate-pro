
EduHacks AI Fest 2025 is a two-week global, fully online hackathon (September 27 – October 11, 2025) hosted on Devpost. Our mission is to empower the next generation of innovators to harness artificial intelligence (AI) to revolutionize education and make learning more accessible, personalized, and engaging.

Tracks & Themes
Participants can choose to innovate in areas such as:

Accessibility in Education: Designing tools to help students with diverse learning needs.

Personalized Learning: Creating AI-driven systems that adapt to individual learners.

Collaborative Tools: Building platforms that make group learning more interactive and effective.

Gamification & Engagement: Using AI to make learning more fun and motivating.

Open-Ended: Any subject related to education.

Workshops & Learning Opportunities
In addition to the competition, EduHacks offers exclusive workshops covering topics such as:

Applying AI in education-focused projects

UI/UX design and prototyping for edtech

Pitching and presenting your ideas effectively

Get Started
Register on Devpost and fill out the Google Form and join our discord community.

Form a team (solo participation or teams of up to 4 are welcome).

Attend workshops to learn tools, frameworks, and techniques.

Develop your project over the 2-week period with mentor support.

Submit your project by October 11, 2025 for judging.

Requirements
What to Build
A project that uses artificial intelligence (AI) to improve education, accessibility, or the learning experience.

Submissions can be apps, websites, platforms, prototypes, or tools — creativity and innovation are encouraged.

Projects must be original and created during the hackathon period (September 27 – October 11, 2025).

Teams may use existing open-source libraries, APIs, or frameworks, but the core idea and implementation must be new.

What to Submit
Each team must submit the following through Devpost:

Project Description: Clear explanation of the problem, your solution, and how AI is applied.

Demo Video (3–5 minutes): A walkthrough showing your project in action (uploaded to YouTube/Vimeo and linked).

GitHub/Code Repository OR Working Demo Link: Source code with a README explaining how to run your project. If only submitting a demo link, the website must reference Eduhacks.

Title & tagline

Team members

Screenshots or visuals of the project

Links to live demo (if applicable)

Devpost Submission Page:

Workshop Integration (Optional): Mention if you applied techniques/tools from EduHacks workshops in your project.

Note: Cross submissions are allowed and will not result in the loss of any points
